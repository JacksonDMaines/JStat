
## General Rules
Constants can be pulled out
Derivative of constant is 0
Derivative of sum/difference of functions is just the sum/difference of derivatives

## Typical Derivative
Let $f(x)=x^a$ where $a\in\mathbb{z}/{\{0\}}$
then

$$
	\frac{d}{dx}f(x) = f'(x) = ax^{a-1}
$$
# Basic Rules
Basic Derivate Rules

## Product Rule
Let $f(x)$ and $g(x)$ functions exists then

$$
\frac{d}{dx}[f(x)g(x)] = f'(x)g(x) + g'(x)f(x)
$$

## Quotient Rule
Let $f(x)$ and $g(x)$ functions exists then

$$
\frac{d}{dx}\left[ \frac{f(x)}{g(x)} \right] = \frac{g(x)f'(x) - f(x)g'(x)}{[g(x)]^2}
$$
a trick to remember this is

$$
\frac{\text{"low d high minus high d low"}}{\text{low squared}}
$$

## Chain Rule
Let $f(x)$ and $g(x)$ functions exists then

$$
\frac{d}{dx}[f(g(x))] = f'(g(x))\cdot g'(x)
$$
