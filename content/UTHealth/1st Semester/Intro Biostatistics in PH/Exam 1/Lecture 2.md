Depending of the [[Lecture 0, 1#Types of Variables|type of data]] we use different summary techniques. 
# Categorical Data
Names for different groups, levels, or categories

**One Var Summary**
- Tables
- Bar charts

Counts of variables = **frequency**
Proportion of levels = **relative frequency**
$$
\text{relative frequency}=\frac{\text{\# of samples in given level}}{\text{total \# of samples}}
$$
**Cumulative frequency**
	sum of current level and previous levels
**Cumulative relative frequency**
$$
=\frac{\text{Cumulative frequency}}{total \# of samples}
$$

Note: Typically you report both summary table and graph at same time. (maybe important for final project)

**Two Var Summary**
- 2 way tables (contingency tables)

# Numerical Data
## Numerical Summaries
### Location based
- **Mean**
$$
\text{Sample Mean}=\bar{x} = \frac{x_{1}+x_{2} \dots + x_{n}}{n}
$$
Population Mean: $\mu$
$\bar{x}$ Estimate $\mu$

- **Median**
	- Sort data, find most middle number
- **Mode**
	- Most common values
	- Can be multiple
- **Quantile**
	- Quartiles
		- Split data into 4 equal parts
		- $Q_{1},Q_{2},Q_{3}$ indicate the cutoff points in the data
	
	- Percentiles 
		- Not Percentages

### Spread Based
- **Variance**
- **Standard deviation**
Square root of variance
- **Interquartile range**

### Graphs
- **Boxplots**
- **Histograms**
