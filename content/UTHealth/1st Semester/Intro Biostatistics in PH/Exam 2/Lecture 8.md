# Point Estimation

- **Population:** 
	- Group we want to study
- **Random Sample:**
	- A subset of population chosen independently
- **Parameter:**
	- A numerical quantity representing a characteristic of the population
-  **Sample Statistics:**
	- Numerical function of sample
	- **Point Estimate:**
		- Estimate of parameter

## Variability In Estimates
- **Standard Error (SE):**
	- Is [[UTHealth/1st Semester/Intro Biostatistics in PH/Exam 1/Lecture 3#Sample Deviation|standard deviation]] of sampling distribution of a sample statistics 
	- Measures the precision of the sample statistics
- **Standard Error of Sample Mean:**
	- measures sample to sample variability of $\bar{X}$ 
	- Denoted: $\sigma_{\bar{X}}$, $SE(\bar{X})$, or $SE_{\bar{X}}$ 
$$
SE_{\bar{X}} = \frac{s}{\sqrt{ n }}
$$
## Central Limit Theorem
- If a sample consist of at least 30 independent observation and the data is not heavily skewed then the distribution of the point estimate can be approximated by a normal model
