- Single sample inference with Normal Dist.

## Inference with Normal Dist.
- CLT (Approx Z-Test)
	- Independent
	- $n>30$
	- not skewed
- Exact Z-Test Case
	- Independent
	- Normally dist.
	- Population $\sigma^2$ known 
		- Unrealistic

- Confidence Interval 
	- $100\left( 1- \frac{\alpha}{2} \right)\%$


# Process of Test
- Formulate hypothesis
	- Null, alternative hypothesis
- Calculate test statistic
- Calculate p-value base on $H_{0}$ and test stat.
- Conclude

## Test Statistic 
$$
t = \frac{\bar{X}-\mu_{0}}{\frac{s}{\sqrt{ n }}}
$$
- Both Sided: $2\cdot P(Z>|t|)$
- One Sided: $P(Z>t)$ or $P(Z<t)$
