# Confidence Interval
- A plausible range of point estimate

$$
(\text{Point Estimate}) \pm(\text{Margin of Error})
$$
**Margin of Error**
$$
MOE=(\text{Critical Value})\times(\text{SE of Point Estimate})
$$

The Confidence Interval can be constructed as followed:
$$
[(\text{Point Estimate})-(\text{Critical Value})\times(\text{SE of Point Estimate}), (\text{Point Estimate})+(\text{Critical Value})\times(\text{SE of Point Estimate})]
$$
The Critical value can be determined by the confidence level

### Interpretation
- If many samples were drawn from a population, and a confidence interval were calculated from each one, approximately ##% of those intervals would contain the population mean 𝜇. 

**Ex:**
- We are 95% confident that the average of weight falls within "lower end" and "upper end".
