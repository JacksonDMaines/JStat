# t-Dist.
- independence
- data comes from nearly normal dist. with mean $\mu$ and SD $\sigma$ 

- thicker tail than normal
- symmetric
- centered at $0$

$$
t = \frac{\bar{x}-\mu}{\frac{s}{\sqrt{ n }}}\sim t_{df}
$$

**Note:** Shape of t-dist varies by degree of freedom
- Degree of freedom will vary by the size of sample
- Critical values for t-dist vary by df

## Degree of Freedom
$$
df = n-1
$$
## Critical Value
$$
P(t_{df}\leq t^*) = 1- \frac{\alpha}{2}
$$
## Confidence Interval
$$
\bar{x}\pm t^*\cdot \frac{s}{\sqrt{ n }}
$$
